 Fast forward two years and Maya's on to grad school, so I've taken over the president slot! SWiCS works to create a more inclusive environment on UVM's campus for women in computer science. As a club, we've done some really cool things! Some of my favorites that I've lead have been creating fundraising for $5,000 scholarship for female and non-binary CS students at UVM, writing a grant and receiving $16,000 in funding to send 8 students to the Women of Silicon Valley Conference in San Francisco, and co-operating with local high schools and tech businesses to bridge the gap between younger girls interested in tech, to colleged aged people, to professionals in the computer science, tech, and engineering fields! One of the thing's I'll miss most about UVM is the community that we've been able to find and build through SWiCS and I hope this is something I can find in a future job! But this website isn't about SWiCS, it's about me! But you can find the SWiCS website here...

 Hi! I'm Anja and welcome to my website! I am a senior at the University of Vermont (UVM) studying Computer Science & Informtation Systems in the College of Engineering and Mathematical Sciences and the Honors College. Growing up, I lived in Montpelier, VT, the capital of Vermont, but I am now located in Burlington, VT for school!

 I've always been the type of person who loves school, I love sitting down and doing homework projects, sometimes the most exciting things is when you finally are able to turn in a project that brought you on such a journey! So in most of my free time, I find myself doing some sort of activity or working on a project. It's hard for me to do nothing, I'm actually writing this on vacation because I'm kind of needing to do something slightly brain worky ( don't get me wrong, this is a wonderful vacation, I'm just quite excited for school to start and to get into the swing of a schedule again haha).

 So, all that being said, outside of classes, I like to do other school related things. My favorite being my time spent as the president of the Society of Women in Computer Science (or SWiCS) at UVM. When I arrived on campus sophomore year after my year at home due to the pandemic, I ended up joining SWiCS, which was working on rising from the ashes of inactivity with the guidance of my friend Maya. I quickly asked her if I could help and ended up becoming vice president.
